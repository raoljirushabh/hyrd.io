Hyrd.me

An open-source, AI-powered job application assistant that rewrites your resume, writes your cover letter, and answers application questions - in a way that sounds like you wrote it, not a machine.
NOTE: It does NOT invent keywords, bullets or examples on the resume. It simply optimizes it.

The problem
Job applications are broken

Most people send the same resume to every job. The ones who do tailor it usually end up with something that reads like it was written by a corporate chatbot. ATS systems reject qualified candidates before a human ever reads their name, and the tools built to help have made the writing worse.

Hyrd was built to fix that. Not by gaming the system, but by doing what a great career coach would do: taking what you have, understanding what the role actually needs, and finding the honest overlap between the two - written in plain, direct human language.

Why this exists
The need for Hyrd

The job market has quietly become a numbers game that punishes good candidates. Companies receive hundreds of applications for a single opening. Most never get read by a person. The first filter is software, and that software is looking for specific words, not talent.

At the same time, the advice candidates get has not kept up. Career coaches cost hundreds of dollars per session. Resume writing services churn out templates. AI tools produce output so obviously machine-written that recruiters have started flagging it on sight.

🤖
Existing AI tools make it worse
ChatGPT and similar tools produce resumes full of em dashes, "leveraged synergies", and uniform bullet structures. Recruiters and AI detectors both flag this instantly. A resume that screams AI-written is often worse than the original.
💸
Professional help is out of reach for most people
A good resume writer charges between $300 and $1,000. Career coaches charge by the hour. Most job seekers, especially early-career candidates and people between jobs, simply cannot afford that.
🎯
Tailoring works, but nobody has time to do it right
Tailored applications significantly outperform generic ones. But doing it properly for a single application takes two to three hours. Applying to ten roles means twenty to thirty hours of work before a single interview. That is not realistic.
🔍
Candidates do not know what ATS systems are looking for
Most people have never seen an ATS score for their own resume. They do not know which keywords they are missing, which sections carry the most weight, or why a role they are qualified for keeps going nowhere.
The resume is not a biography. It is a relevance argument. Every word should be doing work to answer one question: why is this person the right fit for this specific role.
The principle behind Hyrd
Objective
What Hyrd is trying to do
Maximum ATS match
Every keyword, responsibility, and requirement in the job description gets addressed somewhere in the resume. The AI audits coverage line by line and fills gaps.
Zero AI tells
No em dashes. No "Furthermore". No "Leveraged synergies". A long banned-words list is baked into every generation. Output has to pass both human readers and AI detectors.
STAR format, invisibly
New bullets are written in Situation/Action/Result format but structured so the format disappears. They read as natural recollections, not templates someone filled in.
Application Q&A
Paste in any application questions and get answers grounded in your actual background and the specific role, not generic interview-prep boilerplate.

Scope
What is and is not in scope
In scope
Resume tailoring, cover letter generation, application Q&A, ATS scoring, PDF and DOCX upload, TXT and DOCX export, keyword audit, STAR bullet generation, grammar and consistency checking.
Out of scope
Job searching, application tracking, interview scheduling, LinkedIn integration, and fabricating credentials or experience. Hyrd enhances what is already there. It does not invent.

Infra

There are no databases, no user accounts, and no stored data. Everything runs in memory for the duration of a session. The only external call is to the Anthropic API, routed through a serverless proxy so the API key never reaches the browser.

Open source
Available on GitHub

Hyrd is fully open source under the MIT license. The entire codebase is a single React component plus a one-file Vercel serverless proxy. There are no proprietary dependencies, no locked APIs beyond Anthropic, and no build-time configuration beyond an environment variable for the API key.
